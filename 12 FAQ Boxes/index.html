frequently Asked Questions

Q: What is artificial intelligence?

A: Artificial Intelligence (AI) refers to the development of computer systems that can perform tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as learning, problem-solving, and decision-making.

Q: What are the different types of AI?

A: AI can be categorized into three types: narrow or weak AI, which is designed for a specific task; general or strong AI, which can understand, learn, and apply its intelligence to various tasks; and artificial superintelligence, an AI that surpasses human intelligence.

Q: How is machine learning different from AI?

A: Machine learning is a subset of AI that focuses on the development of algorithms allowing systems to learn and improve from data without explicit programming. AI encompasses broader concepts beyond just learning from data.

Q: What are the ethical concerns surrounding AI?

A: Ethical concerns with AI include issues related to bias in algorithms, data privacy, job displacement due to automation, and the potential misuse of advanced AI technologies.

Q: How is AI being used in various industries?

A: AI is employed across industries like healthcare (for diagnostics and personalized medicine), finance (for fraud detection and trading), automotive (for autonomous vehicles), retail (for personalized recommendations), and many others to improve efficiency and decision-making processes.
